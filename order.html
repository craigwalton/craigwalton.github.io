Product Specification

Stair Hook is designed to fix onto vertical poles of diameter 20mm-30mm which are approximately the diameters of a 1 pence and 2 pound coin. It is designed to support bicycles by the saddle rail. It could be used to support bikes by the frame, but brake and gear cables may get in the way.

Installation

  1. Always follow the Safety instructions included and displayed below.
  2. Wrap provided rubber material tightly around pole.
  3. Bring Stair Hook and best-fitting Clasp together, around the rubber. Make sure the two nuts are fitted into the clasp you're using.
  4. Use a Phillips (or Pozidriv) screwdriver to tighten the bolts. Should be hand tight (do not over-tighten).

Safety

Before using Stair Hook:
  1. Ensure your bike weighs less than 20kg. Stair Hook has been designed to support bikes up to a maximum of 20kg.
  2. Ensure your bike's saddle rails are exposed for engagement with the hook. Ensure the saddle rails stay engaged with the hook if the bike naturally tilts forward or backward when suspended. If in doubt, do not use.
  3. Make sure that the saddle and seat post is securely fastened to the bike frame.
  4. Check the balusters (banister poles) of your staircase are structurally sound and firmly attached to the staircase.
  5. Check that the baluster (banister pole) is between 20mm and 30mm in diameter and use the supplied rubber material to ensure sufficient grip is achieved.
Whilst using Stair Hook:
  1. For added safety, as well as suspending it from the saddle on Stair Hook, always lock your bike with a U-lock (aka D-lock) to a baluster (banister pole) other than the one Stair Hook is mounted on.
  2. Whilst it is possible to fix Stair Hook to any floor with a stair, only the ground floor is recommended due to the risk of accidentally dropping the bike and causing damage to property or injury.
  3. If any damage including visible deformation is observed on the product, do not use. If in doubt, get in touch.
  4. Lift the bike up and over the hook to remove - do not pull or twist the bike in such a way that could cause damage to the hook.
  5. Do not expose to temperatures significantly above room temperature. Max 40C.
